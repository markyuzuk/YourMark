Frequently Asked Questions

Clear, honest answers to the questions we hear most often.

What makes Sensorium different?

Most networks are corporate and distant. Sensorium is a 'human-scale' network. We provide the resources of a large organization but preserve the local heart of your doctor's office. You'll be cared for by local teams who live in your community and understand your needs.

Who will I see at my visits?

You'll be cared for by a licensed local physician and a dedicated research team. You'll also have a Patient Concierge—your personal point of contact who helps coordinate appointments, answers questions, and ensures you feel supported throughout your participation.

Do I have to leave my regular doctor?

No. We work alongside your primary care physician to ensure your health remains the top priority. Your regular doctor will be kept informed about your participation, and you'll continue your normal care.

Is it safe?

Your safety is our first priority. Every study is reviewed by independent oversight committees (IRBs) and monitored closely by our medical staff. All studies are conducted under strict FDA and IRB guidelines with informed consent.

Safety measures include: Regular monitoring, immediate reporting of side effects, independent oversight, and the ability to withdraw at any time.

Can I change my mind?

Always. Participation is 100% voluntary. You can withdraw at any time, for any reason, without penalty or loss of benefits to which you're otherwise entitled.

What are the benefits of participating?

You gain access to cutting-edge treatments, receive expert medical care at no cost, and contribute to advancing medicine for future generations. Many participants also find meaning in knowing they're helping others who may face similar health challenges.

How much time will it take?

Time commitment varies by study. Some require weekly visits, others monthly. Your Patient Concierge will explain the complete schedule before you enroll so you can make an informed decision about whether it fits your life.

Will I be compensated?

Many studies provide compensation for your time and travel expenses. Specific details vary by study. We'll always be transparent about compensation before you enroll.

What if I have side effects?

Your research team monitors you closely throughout the study. Any side effects are documented and addressed immediately by qualified medical professionals. You'll have 24/7 access to our medical team if you have concerns.

How do I know if I'm eligible?

Each study has specific criteria based on factors like age, health condition, medical history, and current medications. Contact us or browse available studies to see which ones match your profile. We'll help you understand eligibility during your initial screening.

Understanding Clinical Trials

Clinical trials are research studies that test new medical approaches in people. They're how we discover better treatments and improve healthcare for everyone.

What Happens in a Clinical Trial?

Screening: We check if you meet the study criteria and answer all your questions.

Informed Consent: You receive detailed information and decide if you want to participate.

Study Visits: Regular appointments for treatment, monitoring, and check-ups.

Follow-up: Continued monitoring even after the study ends.

Study Phases Explained

Phase I: Tests safety in a small group (20-80 people).

Phase II: Tests effectiveness and side effects (100-300 people).

Phase III: Compares to standard treatments (300-3,000 people).

Phase IV: Long-term safety after FDA approval.

Most Sensorium studies are Phase II-IV.

Your Rights as a Participant

These rights are protected by federal law and overseen by independent review boards.

  • Right to Full Information

    You have the right to know all details about the study before deciding to participate, including potential risks, benefits, and alternatives.

  • Right to Voluntary Participation

    Participation is completely voluntary. You can withdraw at any time, for any reason, without penalty.

  • Right to Privacy

    Your privacy and medical information will be protected under HIPAA and other federal regulations.

  • Right to Ask Questions

    All questions will be answered by qualified medical staff, at any time during your participation.

  • Right to Compensation

    You'll be compensated for your time and travel expenses as outlined in the study protocol.
